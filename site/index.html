№ 6 — questions

Fair questions

Where are my identities stored?

Nowhere. They're re-computed from SHA-256(seed : site) every time you look at them. The only stored things are your seed, your settings, and the list of sites you've filled — all in local extension storage on your machine.

Is the password any good?

16 characters, four character classes, unique per site, recoverable from your seed. But Burner is not a password manager — no breach alerts, no sync, no vault. Use it for accounts you don't care about. That's the whole product.

Can sites tell it's fake?

Fields are set through native setters with real input events, so it looks like typing. But the phone number won't pass SMS verification and the email won't receive mail in fake mode — by design. Burner is for the "why do you even need this" forms, not for identity verification.

Is this… allowed?

Giving a made-up name to a newsletter wall is fine. Fraud, KYC, payments, or anything with legal weight is not what this is for, and not what it's built to do. Be an adult.

Firefox? Web Store?

Both on the roadmap. Today: Chrome, Brave, Edge and Arc via Load unpacked — takes 30 seconds, instructions below.
